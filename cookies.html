1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device — computer, smartphone, or tablet — when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and give website owners information about how their site is being used. Cookies are stored locally on your device and can be read by the website that placed them when you return.

In addition to traditional cookies, we also use similar technologies such as web beacons (also known as pixel tags or clear GIFs), local storage objects, and session storage. Web beacons are tiny image files embedded in web pages or emails that allow us to determine whether an action has occurred. Local storage and session storage are web storage technologies that allow websites to store data in a browser, functioning similarly to cookies but with different size limits and persistence characteristics.

Cookies can be "session cookies" (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent cookies" (remaining on your device for a defined period or until you delete them). They may be placed by us ("first-party cookies") or by third-party services we use ("third-party cookies"). By using the 100CLAN platform, you consent to the use of cookies and similar technologies as described in this Cookie Policy.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies for several important purposes. First, for authentication and security: cookies help us identify you when you log in, keep your session active as you navigate between pages, and protect your account from unauthorized access. Without these cookies, you would need to log in again with every page you visit. Security-related cookies also help us detect suspicious activity and protect both you and the platform.

Second, we use cookies to remember your preferences. This includes your preferred language (English or Arabic), display settings, notification preferences, and other personalization choices you have made on the platform. These preference cookies ensure that you do not have to reset your choices every time you visit 100CLAN, creating a more seamless and personalized experience.

Third, we use analytics cookies to understand how visitors use our platform. This data — aggregated and anonymized — tells us which pages are most popular, how users navigate through the site, where they encounter problems, and how we can improve the overall user experience. Finally, we use marketing cookies to measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns, serve relevant ads to potential users on third-party platforms, and track conversions from those ads back to our platform.

4. Third-Party Cookies

We use a number of third-party services on the 100CLAN platform that may set their own cookies on your device. These include Google Analytics, which we use to analyze platform traffic and user behavior in aggregate. Google Analytics places the _ga cookie and may set additional cookies depending on your interaction with the platform. Google's use of this data is governed by Google's Privacy Policy, which can be reviewed at policies.google.com/privacy.

We also use the Facebook Pixel, a piece of code that allows us to measure the effectiveness of our advertising by understanding the actions people take on our website. Facebook may use this data to deliver targeted ads to users of the Facebook and Instagram platforms. Facebook's data practices are governed by Meta's Data Policy, available at facebook.com/policy.php. You can opt out of Facebook's personalized advertising through your Facebook Ad Preferences settings.

We use Intercom to provide in-app customer support and chat functionality. Intercom sets the intercom-id cookie to identify users across support sessions and provide a continuous support experience. Intercom's privacy practices are described in their Privacy Policy at intercom.com/legal/privacy. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these third-party services and encourage you to review their respective policies.

5. Managing Cookies

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies (other than strictly essential cookies, which are required for the platform to function). Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can usually find these settings in the "Privacy," "Security," or "Settings" menu of your browser. Common browser cookie controls include the ability to delete existing cookies, block third-party cookies, allow cookies from specific sites only, and be notified when new cookies are set.

To manage cookies in popular browsers: in Google Chrome, go to Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and Other Site Data. In Mozilla Firefox, go to Preferences → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data. In Safari, go to Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data. In Microsoft Edge, go to Settings → Privacy, Search, and Services → Cookies and Site Permissions. Note that restricting certain cookies may affect the functionality and personalization of the 100CLAN platform. For example, disabling session cookies will prevent you from staying logged in.

For analytics cookies specifically, you can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on. For interest-based advertising, you can opt out through the Digital Advertising Alliance's opt-out page or the Your Online Choices tool if you are located in Europe. Mobile device users can control ad tracking through device settings (Limit Ad Tracking on iOS, or Opt Out of Interest-Based Ads on Android).
